* PBX ideas

Loose brainstorm of directions to take the home PBX. Nothing here is
committed work — pick whatever sounds fun. Each idea names the Asterisk
piece (module / config file / extension number) it would touch, so the
"is this an evening or a weekend?" answer is roughly visible at a glance.

Numbering convention reused from =pjsip.conf=: =6001-6009= softphones,
=6010-6019= ATAs, =6020-6029= mobile. Feature codes already in use:
=*43= (echo), =*44= (MoH), =*97= (voicemail), =800/801/802= (rotary
aliases). Anything below picks a code that doesn't collide.

** Calling features (dialplan-level)

*** Call parking

=*70= parks the active call in a numbered slot; anyone on any phone
can pick it up by dialling the slot number. Needs =res_parking.so=
loaded plus a =res_parking.conf= block defining the parking lot. Great
for "I'll grab this on the office softphone" without putting the
caller on hold.

*** Pickup groups

=*8= answers a phone that is ringing somewhere else in the house —
useful when the kitchen ATA rings and you're at the desk. Add
=named_pickup_group=family= to the endpoint template in =pjsip.conf=
and an extension that calls =PickupChan(PJSIP/${EXTEN})= (or just
=Pickup()= for unscoped pickup).

*** Ring / hunt groups

Dial a single number (e.g. =600=) and have several phones ring at
once, first to answer wins. Pure dialplan:
=Dial(PJSIP/6001&PJSIP/6010&PJSIP/6011,30)=. Sequential hunt (next
phone after each timeout) is a slightly more involved variant using
=Queue= or chained =Dial= calls.

*** Intercom / auto-answer paging

Dial =*80<ext>= and the target phone auto-answers on speaker. Built
into Asterisk as =Page()=; for the Grandstream HT812 it relies on the
=Call-Info: answer-after=0= header (enable "Allow Auto-Answer by
Call-Info" on the ATA). Set up once, then it's a free intercom across
the whole house.

*** All-phones page

Dial =*81= to talk through every phone at once ("dinner is ready!").
=Page(PJSIP/6001&PJSIP/6010&PJSIP/6011,d)= — the =d= flag makes it
duplex if you want talk-back, omit it for announce-only. Combine with
a SIP paging horn in §10 for whole-house coverage.

*** Do Not Disturb

=*78= toggles DND on the calling extension, =*79= clears it. Store
state in =AstDB=, check it from the =_6XXX= rule before dialling. Per
extension, persists across reloads — much nicer than the
per-handset DND on the HT812.

*** Blocklist / VIP routing

Match =${CALLERID(num)}= against an =AstDB= list. Blocked numbers go
to =Congestion()= (caller hears fast-busy); VIPs get a distinctive
ring or skip straight to the mobile. Only really useful once a SIP
trunk is in place and PSTN spam can actually reach you.

*** Speed-dial out

=9XX= shortcuts to frequently-called external numbers (grandparents,
takeaway). Same pattern as =_6XXX=, but the dialled number is looked
up in a small table and =Dial=ed out the trunk instead of in-house.

** Auto-attendant & time conditions

*** IVR welcome menu

Outside callers hit "Press 1 for kitchen, 2 for office, # for
voicemail". =Background()= to play the prompt, =WaitExten()= to
collect the digit, then route accordingly. Record the prompts via a
small =Record()= extension or drop pre-cooked WAVs into
=/var/lib/asterisk/sounds/custom/=.

*** Quiet-hours routing

=GotoIfTime()= skips ringing the ATAs after 22:00 and routes straight
to voicemail. Holidays are the same construct, one block per holiday
date. Pairs nicely with a "we're sleeping, leave a message"
time-of-day greeting.

*** Recorded announcements

=Playback()= a custom greeting before ringing the phones — "you've
reached the Stigo household". Trivial to add to any inbound route,
nice touch for friends-and-family inbound calls.

** Voicemail upgrades

*** Voicemail-to-email

Add =email==, =attach=yes=, and =format=wav49|gsm= per mailbox in
=voicemail.conf=. Each new message arrives in your inbox with the
audio attached. Needs an SMTP relay reachable from the container —
=msmtp= sidecar pointing at a real mail host is the simplest.

*** Voicemail transcription

Pipe the WAV through =whisper.cpp= (local) or a cloud STT and append
the transcript to the email body. Hook via
=externnotify=/usr/local/bin/vm-transcribe.sh= in =voicemail.conf= —
Asterisk runs the script after each save, you get back text in
seconds.

*** MWI on the analogue phones

The HT812 supports stutter dial-tone (and the message-waiting LED, if
the handset has one) for waiting voicemails — confirm "Subscribe for
MWI" is enabled on the ATA. The PBX already publishes MWI events;
this is a one-checkbox win on the ATA side.

*** Time-of-day outgoing greetings

Different IVR greeting before / after hours, swap with =GotoIfTime=.
Same mechanism as quiet-hours routing, just on the prompt side.

** PSTN / talking to the outside world

*** SIP trunk

Sign up with a VoIP provider (Telnyx, voip.ms, sipgate, 46elks for SE
numbers) to get a real phone number. Inbound DID rings the IVR or a
ring group; outbound =9XXXXXX= routes through the trunk. If the trunk
requires it, set =external_media_address= /
=external_signaling_address= in =pjsip.conf= so SDP/Contact headers
carry the public IP.

*** Multiple DIDs per person

Give each family member their own external number on the trunk, route
each DID to that person's extension. Most providers charge around €1
per number per month — cheap way to give the kids their own phone
number for school admin.

*** Emergency dialling caveat

VoIP trunks generally cannot reach SE 112 / US 911 reliably (no
guaranteed location data, no SLA). Worth documenting once and keeping
a mobile in the house as the emergency fallback. Some trunks support
E911 in the US for an extra fee; check before relying on it.

*** Fax via T.38

=udptl.conf= is already loaded. Pair with =res_fax_spandsp.so= to
send/receive PDFs as faxes, or with a fax-capable ATA (HT813 has FXO,
HT812 is FXS-only). Almost no real-world use left in 2026, but
satisfying to have working.

*** Call forwarding to mobile

When 6010 doesn't answer in 20 s, fall through to your cellular
number via the trunk instead of voicemail. =Dial(PJSIP/6010,20)= then
=Dial(PJSIP/trunk/+46701234567,20)= then voicemail as final fallback.
Two-line dialplan change once a trunk exists.

** Conferencing

*** ConfBridge room

=8000= enters a permanent conference bridge. Family members dial in
for a five-minute "everyone home for dinner?" sync.
=confbridge.conf= defines profiles — mute/unmute, recording, MoH
while waiting for the host, etc.

*** PIN-protected bridge

Same idea but =Authenticate()= prompts for a code first. Useful the
moment any extension becomes reachable from outside Tailscale.

*** Scheduled standing call

A cron job uses AMI =Originate= to dial every family extension into
the bridge at 18:00 on Sundays. The phones ring, the family picks up,
everyone is already on the same call — no "are you there yet?"
fumbling.

** Provisioning (zero-touch phone setup)

*** HTTP provisioning for Grandstream

Host an XML config at =http://asterisk/provision/cfg<MAC>.xml=. New
HT812s and GXP/GRP desk phones fetch their config on boot — no more
clicking through 40 web-UI fields per device (see [[file:HT812.org][HT812.org]] for
how tedious that currently is). Big quality-of-life win for adding
the next phone.

*** Templated provisioning server

An =nginx= sidecar serving per-MAC config blobs rendered from a
template. Secrets injected at render time, so the git repo never
holds plaintext SIP passwords. Stretch goal: serve over HTTPS with a
client-cert check so a stolen MAC alone isn't enough.

*** Per-device password generation

Replace the placeholder convention (username == password,
=";; change me"= comments in =pjsip.conf=) with random 20-character
passwords stored in =pass= / =age= / sops, injected at provision
time. Finally retires those TODOs.

** Programmability (AMI / ARI / Stasis)

*** AMI listener

Subscribe to call events from a small Python or Go script via the
Asterisk Manager Interface — log who called whom, push a desktop
notification when the doorbell rings, light up a Hue bulb on inbound
calls. =manager.conf= is already in the repo, just needs a user
configured.

*** ARI / Stasis app

Write a real call-control app in Python (=ari-py=) or Node. Example:
an "ask the LLM" extension — dial =*99=, speak, transcript goes to
Claude / a local model, response gets TTS'd back through the call.
Modern replacement for AGI; clean HTTP/WebSocket API, easy to
sandbox.

*** Originate from shell

=asterisk -rx 'channel originate PJSIP/6010 extension 6001@internal'=
rings two phones together from any script. Building block for the
home-automation and novelty ideas below — any cron job, webhook, or
sensor can now make the phones ring.

*** Webhook on call event

A tiny AMI-to-webhook bridge so n8n / Home Assistant / Discord get a
POST every time a call starts or ends. Suddenly the PBX is a citizen
of the rest of the home-automation stack.

** Home automation hooks

*** Home Assistant SIP integration

HA can register as a SIP endpoint via its =voip= integration. Dial
=*123= to trigger an HA scene/script via dialplan + =System()=, or do
it cleanly through a Stasis app. Two-way: HA can also originate calls
("doorbell pressed → call every phone").

*** Doorbell rings every phone

A SIP doorbell (Grandstream GDS3710, DoorBird, 2N Verso) registers
as a regular endpoint. Inbound call from it pages every phone with a
distinctive ring; two-way audio plus DTMF =*= to release the gate.
Pairs with the all-phones page above.

*** Motion-triggered intercom

HA detects motion in the nursery and originates a one-way auto-answer
call from a nursery ATA mic to the parents' softphone. Same
=Call-Info: answer-after=0= trick as the paging idea — "are they
awake?" without walking down the corridor.

*** "I'm home" announcement

Phone connects to home WiFi → HA triggers =Page()= to the kitchen ATA
playing a welcome jingle. Pointless, delightful, two lines of YAML on
the HA side plus an =Originate=.

** Fun / novelty extensions

*** Speaking clock

Dial =*61= for a TTS'd current time ("it's twenty past three").
=SayUnixTime()= is built into the dialplan — no external dependency,
ten-line extension, immediate dopamine.

*** Weather hotline

=*62= fetches the local forecast (SMHI / met.no), pipes it through
Piper TTS, and =Playback()=s the result. Refresh the WAV on a 30-min
cron so the call itself is instant rather than waiting on an HTTP
fetch.

*** Internet radio

=*63= bridges the call into a streaming radio station via
=MP3Player()= or a local Icecast pull. The kitchen ATA becomes a
radio — pick up the handset, press =*63=, leave it on the counter.

*** Bedtime story for the kids

=*64<n>= plays story =n= from =/var/lib/asterisk/sounds/stories/= and
auto-hangs-up at end of file. Genuinely useful while making dinner;
the kid picks up a phone and listens to a story instead of bothering
you.

*** Baby monitor on demand

Dial =*65= from the parents' phone and the nursery ATA auto-answers
listen-only (mic open, speaker muted). Same Call-Info trick as
paging. No standing connection — only opens when you actually want
to listen.

*** Retro answering-machine MoH

Replace the default MoH with full 1995 cassette-tape hold music. Drop
files into =/var/lib/asterisk/moh/= and =moh reload=. Pure vibes,
zero effort.

*** Speed-dial-to-Spotify

=*7<playlist>= triggers Home Assistant to start a playlist on a
chosen speaker. The PBX is just the trigger — no audio crosses it,
the phone becomes a music remote.

*** Wakeup call

Dial =*66=, enter a time, get called at that time with an alarm tone.
Stores the schedule in =AstDB=; a cron / =at= job =Originate=s the
call. Classic hotel-PBX feature, useful for kids who want a phone to
wake them up.

** Hardware worth considering

*** SIP doorbell with camera

Grandstream GDS3710, DoorBird D11x, 2N Verso. All register as
standard SIP endpoints, so the PBX side is just another phone.
Combine with the doorbell-paging idea — and with HA, you get the
video stream on a tablet at the same time.

*** DECT base for cordless handsets

Gigaset N510 IP PRO is the classic — up to six cordless handsets per
base, all reachable as one SIP account or one per handset. Better
range and battery than a WiFi softphone, and noticeably less laggy
than the mobile-via-Tailscale path.

*** Paging horn / ceiling speaker

Algo 8186, CyberData V3, or any SIP-enabled PA. Combine with
=Page()= for whole-house announcements that don't depend on a phone
being off-hook. Pairs especially well with workshop / garage spaces
where you'd never carry a handset.

*** USB FXO gateway

If you want to keep an actual POTS landline alive on the PBX (rare
in 2026, but possible). Cisco SPA3102 is the cheap classic; gives
you one FXO port to terminate the copper.

*** Conference speakerphone

Polycom IP 5000 or Yealink CP920 in the dining room makes ConfBridge
actually pleasant — full-duplex pickup, decent room mic, doesn't
need an ATA.

*** Wired SIP desk phone

Yealink T31G / T33G / Fanvil X3U are cheap, reliable, and *don't*
need an ATA. Worth considering before adding a third analogue line
to the HT812 — one ethernet cable replaces analogue wiring plus a
power supply.

** Reliability & ops

*** Astdb + spool backups

Nightly =tar= of the =asterisk_lib= and =asterisk_spool= named
volumes to Restic / rclone. The astdb-corruption episode from the
HT812 registration loop is a nice motivator — having voicemails and
=astdb.sqlite3= snapshotted off-box turns a corruption event into a
"restore yesterday's file" five-minute fix.

*** Prometheus exporter

=res_prometheus.so= (Asterisk module) or =asterisk_exporter=
(sidecar) feeds a Grafana dashboard — registrations, active channels,
call counts, RTP loss/jitter. Catches things like "ATA hasn't
re-registered in 30 minutes" before the user notices.

*** CDR analytics

=cdr_custom.conf= is already enabled and writing
=cdr-custom/Master.csv=. Pipe that into Grafana / DuckDB / a tiny
Streamlit dashboard for "who called whom this month". Mostly for
curiosity, occasionally useful for "wait, who answered that?"

*** Log rotation

=logger.conf= rotation plus a =logrotate= sidecar so the
=asterisk_log= volume doesn't grow unbounded. Currently a slow leak;
caught in time, it's a non-issue.

*** Healthcheck → ntfy

Extend the compose healthcheck to push a notification (ntfy /
Pushover / Discord webhook) if Asterisk goes unhealthy. Quieter than
finding out because a phone won't dial.

*** Config-test in CI

=just config-test= already exists locally. Wire it into a GitHub
Action so a broken =pjsip.conf= is caught on push, before it ever
reaches the live container.

** Security

*** fail2ban for SIP

Only relevant once 5060/5061 is exposed to the public internet —
currently Tailscale-only, so not urgent. =security.conf= already
emits the events fail2ban needs; the filter / jail config is the
missing piece.

*** Strong per-device passwords

See provisioning above. The =";; change me"= comments in =pjsip.conf=
are perfectly fine while everything is Tailscale-only, and a real
problem the moment they're not.

*** Internal CA for SIP-TLS

Replace the self-signed cert with one from a small internal CA
(=smallstep step-ca=) or Tailscale-issued certs via =tailscale cert=.
Mobile clients can then validate properly without the "trust unknown
certificate" toggle — both more secure and less annoying to
re-onboard a device.

*** ACLs

=acl.conf= restricting which source IPs can reach each transport —
e.g. the ATAs only from =192.168.1.0/24=, mobile endpoints only via
the Tailscale CGNAT range. Defence in depth on top of strong
passwords.
